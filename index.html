Semantic Engagement

Why does one short video explode and a near-identical one sink? Part of the answer is hiding in the caption — not the hashtags, the meaning. This build predicts engagement from the semantics of the text, turning captions into embeddings and learning what kinds of meaning travel. I rebuilt it from scratch to see how far language alone can explain virality.

What it is

Predicting reach from meaning

Most engagement models lean on surface signals — follower count, posting time, hashtag popularity. This one asks a harder question: can the semantic content of a caption, on its own, predict how a video performs? To answer it, every caption is mapped into a high-dimensional embedding where similar meanings sit close together, and a model learns the relationship between that meaning-space and real engagement numbers.

It's a clean way to study a messy phenomenon. By stripping away the metadata and keeping only the language, you isolate exactly how much of engagement is explained by what was said — and which themes, tones and framings consistently land.

Architecture

How a prediction is made

Every caption travels the same path from text to number:

  1. Collect

    Assemble captions paired with their engagement figures.

  2. Preprocess

    Clean and normalise the text into a consistent form.

  3. Embed

    Encode each caption into a semantic vector with a transformer.

  4. Fit

    Train a regressor to predict engagement from the embedding.

  5. Interpret

    Analyse which regions of meaning-space the model rewards, and report the error honestly.

Reflection

What rebuilding it taught me

  • Embeddings unlock text. Raw captions are unusable to a regressor; a sentence embedding turns meaning into geometry a model can fit.
  • Semantics explains some, not all. Language is a real signal but only part of the story — being honest about the unexplained variance matters.
  • Clustering tells the story. The most interesting output isn't the prediction, it's seeing which themes consistently win.
  • Preprocessing is quietly decisive. How you handle emojis and mentions changes what "meaning" the model even sees.
  • Correlation ≠ causation, loudly. A theme that predicts engagement isn't a recipe to cause it — easy to forget, important to say.
